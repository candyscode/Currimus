CURRIMUS

Support

Currimus records on the Apple Watch. The iPhone app is where you read the log, set your zones and your target race, and export everything.

Starting a run

Open Currimus on the watch and pick Start for a normal run, Trail for climb and elevation, or Pacer to run to a target pace. Tap the run screen — or press both buttons — to pause.

“A run will not start”

Distance and heart rate both come from Apple Health, so a run cannot record without permission to save workouts. If it is refused, the watch says so instead of recording an empty run. To grant it: iPhone › Watch › Privacy › Health › Currimus.

“My run has no route or elevation”

That is location access. A run without it still records distance, pace, heart rate and zones — only the map, the climb and the elevation profile are missing. Grant it under iPhone › Watch › Privacy › Location › Currimus. Runs imported from other apps have no route either; Currimus reads their distance and duration from Health, not their track.

“Distance stays at 0.00 during a run”

Health can grant permission to save workouts while withholding distance separately, and Apple deliberately does not reveal which permissions were given. Currimus shows a banner after two minutes of no distance. Check that Distance is allowed under iPhone › Watch › Privacy › Health › Currimus. A run that ends with no distance is not filed in the log.

“My old runs are not here”

Tap Apple Health in Settings, or “Already have runs? Bring them in” on the welcome screen. Currimus reads running workouts other apps saved to Health and counts them in every total. They have no per-kilometre splits, so they show fewer details than runs Currimus recorded itself.

Fixing a run

  • Delete — press and hold a run in the log. Runs recorded by another app have to be deleted in that app or in Health.
  • Rename, or change road/trail or the session type — open the run and tap Edit run. Distance, time and heart rate are not editable: they were measured.

Battery

GPS is the largest single draw of a recorded run — more than the screen. Settings › GPS accuracy trades precision for hours and says what each step costs. Trail runs are worth keeping on High, since elevation comes from GPS too.

Getting your data out

Settings › Export all runs writes GPX and CSV and hands them to the iOS share sheet. No account, no lock-in, any time.

Still stuck?

Write to support@currimus.app. Please include your iPhone and watch models and their OS versions — it saves a round trip.
